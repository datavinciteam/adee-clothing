How to measure

Use a soft measuring tape. Measure over light clothing or directly on skin, keeping the tape flat, not pulled tight.

Garment Length

Measure from the top of the shoulder seam to the bottom of the foot.

Chest

Wrap the tape around the middle of the chest and note the circumference.

Hip

Wrap the tape around the widest part of the hips and note the circumference.

Weight

Use your baby's current weight as the primary size guide. If near the top of a weight range, size up.

Hat Height

Hold the hat upright and measure from the very top of the hat to the bottom of the brim.

Hat Width

Wrap the tape around the widest part of the hat and note the circumference.

Chest

Measure straight across the chest from one side seam to the other.

Length

Measure from the highest shoulder point straight down to the bottom hem.

Stretchies & Layettes

Month-based sizing, 1M to 18M

Sized primarily by your baby's weight, the most consistent guide in the first year. If your baby is toward the top of a weight range, always size up for more room and longer wear.

Rompers, Undershirts & Bloomers

Age-range sizing, 0-3M to 12-24M

Layette sets include a stretchy, bonnet, and blanket. The bonnet and blanket are one size, only the stretchy follows the size chart. Rompers are available to 2-3 Yrs.

Buying as a gift?

When in doubt, size up

Babies grow faster than anyone expects. A slightly larger piece simply means a few more precious months in it. Browse the full baby collection.

Fit & Sizing

True to size, with one exception

Dresses and tops run true to size for most silhouettes. Smocked and structured occasion dresses have a close, tailored fit. If your daughter is between sizes, always size up.
